What is Voice OTP?

Voice OTP (Voice One-Time Password) is a secure and user-friendly two-factor authentication method that verifies user identity through automated voice calls. Instead of receiving an OTP via SMS, users get a voice call delivering a unique code valid for a short period. They then enter this code to authenticate themselves. Compared to SMS-based OTPs, Voice OTP provides enhanced security, broader accessibility, and greater convenience for users.

How Voice OTP Works?

Voice OTP functions by delivering a one-time password to a user’s mobile device through an automated voice call.

Process:

  • The user inputs their mobile number on a website or app.
  • The system generates a unique OTP and sends it via an automated voice call.
  • The user answers the call and listens to the OTP carefully.
  • The user enters the OTP on the platform to complete identity verification.

Example of a Service that implements Voice OTP

Voice OTPs are widely used by various businesses and industries. Here are some examples:

Banks and Financial Institutions:

Banks, microfinance institutions, and loan companies use voice OTPs to enhance security during transactions and verify user identities.

E-commerce Platforms:

Online retailers and e-commerce websites utilize voice OTPs to confirm order placements, reducing the risk of fraudulent transactions.

Logistics and Delivery Services:

Companies involved in logistics or goods delivery often use voice OTPs for authenticating user identity.

Bill Payment Services:

Bill payment companies employ voice OTPs to verify user accounts and ensure secure transactions.
